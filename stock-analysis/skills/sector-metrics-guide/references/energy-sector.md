# Energy Sector Metrics

## Oil & Gas

**Representative Companies:** XOM, CVX, COP, EOG, PXD

| Metric | Description | Good Level |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Production Growth** | BOE/day increase | 5%+ growth |
| **Reserve Replacement Ratio** | New reserves / Production | 100%+ sustaining |
| **Breakeven Oil Price** | Price needed for profitability | Lower is better |
| **FCF Yield** | FCF / Market cap | 10%+ attractive |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | Leverage level | 2x or below safe |
| **ROIC** | Return on invested capital | 15%+ excellent |

### Key Considerations
- Commodity price exposure is primary driver
- Low breakeven provides downside protection
- Capital discipline crucial for shareholder returns
- ESG pressures affecting long-term strategy

## MLP / Midstream

**Representative Companies:** EPD, ET, WMB, KMI, MPLX

| Metric | Description | Good Level |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **DCF (Distributable Cash Flow)** | Distributable cash flow | Distribution source |
| **Distribution Coverage** | DCF / Distribution | 1.2x+ safe |
| **Contract Revenue %** | Take-or-pay contract share | Higher is more stable |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | Leverage | 4x or below healthy |
| **Volume Growth** | Pipeline throughput | Growing trend |

### Key Considerations
- Fee-based revenue is more stable than commodity-exposed
- Distribution sustainability depends on coverage ratio
- Capex requirements can pressure distributions
- Tax complexity with K-1 forms
